Abstract

This paper presents a versatile paradigm for integrating multi-view normal and reflectance maps obtained through photometric stereo. Our approach utilizes a pixel-wise joint re-parameterization of normal and reflectance, treating them as a vector of luminances rendered under simulated, varying illumination. This re-parameterization facilitates the seamless integration of normal and reflectance maps as input data in neural volume rendering-based 3D-reconstruction, while maintaining a singular optimization objective. This stands in contrast to recent multi-view photometric stereo (MVPS) methods that rely on multiple, potentially conflicting objectives. Despite its apparent simplicity, our proposed approach surpasses state-of-the-art methods on MVPS benchmarks across F-score, Chamfer distance, and mean angular error. Notably, it demonstrates a significant improvement in the detailed 3D-reconstruction of areas with high curvature or low visibility.
